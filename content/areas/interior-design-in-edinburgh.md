Edinburgh's architectural heritage creates both extraordinary opportunities and genuine constraints for interior designers. From the soaring ceilings of New Town townhouses to the compact spaces of Stockbridge tenements, from the contemporary apartments of Quartermile to the renovated warehouses of Leith—each presents unique challenges that demand local knowledge and creative solutions.

## Edinburgh's Distinctive Property Landscape

The Scottish capital possesses one of Europe's best-preserved Georgian townscapes. The New Town's wide streets and elegant crescents house properties with original cornicing, working shutters, and proportions that modern architects can only dream of. Yet these same properties often come with impractical layouts, inadequate storage, and heating systems that struggle against Scottish winters.

Victorian tenements—the workhorses of Edinburgh housing—offer surprising versatility behind their uniform sandstone facades. High ceilings, generous windows, and solid construction provide excellent bones for renovation. But narrow hallways, box rooms, and bathrooms squeezed into former cupboards require thoughtful reconfiguration.

Contemporary developments, from the converted industrial spaces of Leith to the new-build apartments of Fountainbridge, bring different challenges: creating character where none exists, making open-plan layouts feel distinct yet connected, and establishing warmth in spaces dominated by glass and concrete.

## Working With Edinburgh's Light

Edinburgh's northerly latitude means dramatically different light conditions throughout the year. Winter days can feel startlingly short, with meaningful natural light lasting only six or seven hours. Summer brings the opposite extreme—lingering twilight that never quite becomes darkness.

We design for this reality. North-facing rooms, common in Edinburgh's grid-pattern streets, need careful colour selection to feel warm rather than gloomy. Paint colours that work beautifully in southern English light can look entirely different under Edinburgh's softer, cooler illumination.

Artificial lighting becomes crucial. We create layered lighting schemes that can transform spaces from bright and energising for short winter days to warm and ambient for long summer evenings. Edinburgh properties often retain original features—picture rails, ceiling roses—that we can use creatively for concealed lighting that doesn't compromise period character.

## Respecting Edinburgh's Listed Buildings

Many Edinburgh properties carry listed building status or fall within conservation areas. This isn't an obstacle—it's an opportunity. The restrictions that protect Edinburgh's architectural heritage push us toward more thoughtful, creative solutions.

We have extensive experience working within listed building constraints, from full planning applications to knowing which changes fall within permitted development. We understand what Edinburgh City Council's planning department expects and can guide clients through the process of appropriate renovation.

More importantly, we believe these properties deserve respect. When we work on a Georgian townhouse that has stood for 200 years, we're custodians of something precious. Our changes should enhance the building's character, not erase it.

## Edinburgh Neighbourhood Expertise

We work across Edinburgh, with particular expertise in areas where our clients typically live:

**The New Town** presents the most prestigious addresses and the most significant design challenges. These properties command premium prices but often need substantial investment to function for modern life. We've created flexible living spaces within the constraints of Category A listed buildings, added contemporary bathrooms without compromising original features, and developed storage solutions for properties designed when servants handled such practicalities.

**Stockbridge and Inverleith** offer a mix of grand townhouses and characterful tenements, all within walking distance of the city centre. These areas suit families and professionals who want period charm with neighbourhood convenience. Properties here often benefit from garden access—rare in Edinburgh—which we can connect visually to interior spaces.

**Morningside and Bruntsfield** represent Edinburgh's comfortable middle-class heartland, with substantial Victorian villas and well-proportioned tenement flats. These properties typically offer more space than their New Town equivalents at lower prices, allowing budgets to stretch further on interior design.

**Leith and Newhaven** have transformed from industrial areas to desirable addresses, with warehouse conversions and new developments offering contemporary alternatives to period properties. We've helped clients establish character and warmth in these newer spaces, which can feel cold and impersonal without careful attention.

## The Edinburgh Budget Reality

Edinburgh property doesn't come cheap, and clients often face a trade-off between purchase price and renovation budget. We help clients think strategically about this balance—sometimes the property that needs more work offers better long-term value than the one that's "finished" to someone else's taste.

We're experienced at phased projects that prioritise the most impactful changes first. A well-designed kitchen or a properly considered living space transformation can dramatically improve daily life while future budget allows for secondary rooms.

We're also realistic about Edinburgh's contractor market. Good tradespeople are in demand and often booked months ahead. We have established relationships with reliable contractors who understand period properties and deliver consistent quality.

## Sustainability in Edinburgh Properties

Edinburgh's older buildings were designed for a different era—coal fires, gas lighting, servants to maintain everything. Making them work sustainably requires careful consideration.

Solid stone walls can't accommodate conventional insulation without compromising internal dimensions or causing moisture problems. We work with conservation-approved methods that improve thermal performance without damaging historic fabric.

Window replacement in listed buildings is often impossible, but secondary glazing can dramatically improve both thermal and acoustic performance. Edinburgh's busy streets mean traffic noise is a genuine issue for many properties—a concern we address alongside energy efficiency.
